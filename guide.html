Overview
What Elevate Does
Elevate is a Harvard-quality AI résumé builder designed specifically for the Australian job market. It guides you through a structured workflow that mirrors what professional résumé writers do — but in minutes, not days.
💼
LinkedIn Import
Export your LinkedIn as PDF and paste the text, or enter your history manually.
🎯
ATS Optimisation
Keywords from the job description are extracted and woven into your résumé automatically.
💡
Three Smart Prompts
Research, Template, and Discovery prompts guide your customisation at each stage.
🏆
Cert Recommendations
Role-specific certifications ranked by income impact — all available online.
🇦🇺
Australian English
Colour, organise, programme, analyse — correctly spelled throughout.
🖨
Print-Ready PDF
One click prints a clean, formatted résumé document — ready to submit.
⏱
Time to complete: A typical session from start to a finished, print-ready résumé takes 15–25 minutes. Discovery and editing can take longer if you have extensive history.

Step 1 of 5
Build Your Profile
Enter your work history, education, and skills. You can import from LinkedIn or fill in the form manually.
1
Choose your import method
You'll see two tabs at the top: 📎 LinkedIn Export and ✏️ Enter Manually. Most people start with LinkedIn Export to pull in their history quickly, then switch to Manual to complete the details.
2
LinkedIn Export (optional fast-start)
On the LinkedIn Export tab, follow the four on-screen steps:

1. Go to your LinkedIn profile
2. Click More → Save to PDF
3. Open the PDF and press Cmd + A then Cmd + C to copy all text
4. Paste into the text area — Elevate will pre-fill your name, email, and phone

Then click Review & Edit Profile → to switch to manual mode and complete the rest.
3
Fill in Personal Information
Enter your Full Name and Email Address — these two fields are required. Add your phone, LinkedIn URL, and select your Australian city from the dropdown. Your Professional Summary is optional; if left blank, Elevate will generate one for you.
4
Add your Key Skills
Type your skills separated by commas in the Skills field. Examples: Project Management, Agile, Python, Stakeholder Engagement, Tableau. Include both technical and soft skills — aim for 8–15 skills.
5
Add your Work History
Click + Add Role for each position. Fill in job title, company, location, and dates. For each role, add Achievement Bullets — start every bullet with an action verb and include a metric where possible.

✦ Good example: "Increased customer retention by 34% by redesigning the onboarding flow."
✗ Weak example: "Responsible for customer onboarding."
6
Add your Education
Click + Add Education for each qualification. Include your degree name, institution, year of completion, and grade or honours (optional). Australian employers expect your highest qualification listed.
7
Click "Continue to Job Target →"
This button activates once your Name and Email are filled in. All other fields can be completed or edited in the Resume Builder step later.
⚠️
Australian résumé tip: Do not include a photo, date of birth, marital status, or nationality — these are not expected and can introduce unconscious bias in Australian hiring.
🎯
Step 2 of 5
Set Your Target Role
Tell Elevate what job you're applying for. The more detail you provide here, the stronger your ATS optimisation will be.
1
Enter the Target Job Title
Type the exact job title from the listing — for example Senior Product Manager or Data Analyst. This is used to generate your professional summary, find relevant certifications, and pre-fill job board searches. This field is required.
2
Add the Company Name (optional)
If you're applying to a specific company, enter their name. Elevate will include it in your success factors and remind you to research them before interviews. Leave blank if you're building a general-purpose résumé.
3
Select Location and Industry
Choose the Australian city you're targeting and the industry. These are used to pre-fill the job board search links in the final step so you land directly on relevant results.
4
Paste the Job Description (highly recommended)
Copy the full job description from SEEK, LinkedIn, or the company website and paste it into the text area. Elevate analyses it and extracts up to 24 ATS keywords. The keyword count is shown below the text area as you type.

This single step has the biggest impact on your ATS score — recruiters use the same keywords to search résumés, and ATS software filters by them automatically.
5
Click "Analyse Role →"
This activates once a Job Title is entered. Elevate will extract keywords and take you to the three customisation prompts.

Step 3 of 5 — Prompt 1 of 3
Research Prompt
Review the ATS keywords extracted from the job description and confirm which ones to include in your résumé.
1
Review the keyword chips
You'll see up to 24 keyword chips extracted from the job description. Keywords shown with a filled background are selected (included). Keywords with a lighter background are deselected (excluded).
2
Toggle keywords on or off
Click any keyword to toggle it. Include keywords you genuinely have experience with. Deselect any that are irrelevant to your background — forcing irrelevant keywords into a résumé can hurt readability and flag as keyword stuffing.
3
Read the Success Factors
Below the keywords, Elevate lists key success factors for the role — things hiring managers look for. Read these carefully; they'll inform how you phrase your bullets and what you emphasise in your summary.
4
Click "Confirm & Choose Template →"
Your selected keywords are saved and will be woven into the résumé during generation. You can see how many are selected in the hint below the chip panel.
✅
ATS fact: Over 75% of résumés are rejected by ATS software before a human reads them. Selecting the right keywords from the job description is the single most effective thing you can do to pass this filter.
📐
Step 3 of 5 — Prompt 2 of 3
Template Prompt
Choose how your résumé is structured. Different roles and career stages benefit from different formats.
1
Choose a Resume Format
Three formats are available — click the one that best fits your situation:

📅 Chronological — Most recent role first. Best for steady career progression and most ATS systems. Recommended for 90% of applications.

⚡ Hybrid — Skills summary at the top, then chronological experience. Best for career changers or roles requiring specific technical skills upfront.

🎯 Targeted — Every section speaks directly to the role. Best for a single, high-priority application where you want maximum impact.
2
Choose résumé length
The default is a one-page résumé, which is standard in Australia for most roles. Tick Two-page résumé if you have 8 or more years of experience, multiple senior roles, or significant research / publication history. Graduate and early-career résumés should always be one page.
3
Note the Australian English setting
Australian English is applied automatically throughout your résumé — colour, organise, programme, analyse, behaviour, recognise. You don't need to do anything; this is handled for you.
4
Click "Confirm & Discover Experience →"
Your template choice is saved. You'll move to the Discovery Prompt — the most valuable step for uncovering experience you may have overlooked.
📌
Which format should I choose? When in doubt, pick Chronological. It's the most familiar format for Australian recruiters, is parsed most reliably by ATS software, and works well across every industry.
💡
Step 3 of 5 — Prompt 3 of 3
Discovery Prompt
A short conversational interview that surfaces achievements, side projects, and experiences you may not have thought to include — but that are genuinely valuable.
1
Answer each question honestly
You'll be shown up to 6 questions, one at a time. Each has three response options:

Yes — tell me more: Opens a text area for you to describe the experience.
Maybe — skip for now: Moves to the next question without capturing anything.
No: Skips to the next question.
2
Describe your experience in plain language
When you click "Yes", write your experience naturally — don't worry about résumé language yet. Elevate will save your description as a bullet. You can then refine it in the Resume Builder step.

Example input: "I ran a coding bootcamp for 12 kids at my local library for three months. About 8 of them finished."
This becomes a bullet: "Facilitated a 12-week coding programme for youth at a community library, achieving a 67% completion rate."
3
Watch the "Captured Experiences" panel
As you answer questions, captured experiences appear in a panel below the question card. These are automatically added to your most recent role in the résumé builder. You can move or edit them in Step 4.
4
Skip remaining questions if needed
At any point, click Skip remaining questions → in the bottom right to proceed directly to the Resume Builder with what you've captured so far.
💬
Why this matters: Research shows that most professionals underestimate 30–40% of their relevant experience on their résumé. Common overlooked items include: volunteer work, informal mentoring, side projects, community involvement, internal training delivered, and process improvements made but never credited.

Step 4 of 5
Resume Builder
Edit every section of your résumé in the left panel and watch the live preview update in real time. The ATS Score gauge shows how well-optimised your résumé is.
1
Understand the layout
The screen is split into two panels:

Left — Editor Panel: Four tabs (Summary, Experience, Skills, Education) for editing each section.
Right — Preview Panel: A live résumé document that updates as you type, plus the ATS Score gauge at the top.
2
Check and improve your ATS Score
The circular gauge in the top right shows your score from 0–97. Below it are four score bars:

Keywords — % of selected JD keywords found in your résumé
Action Verbs — Strength of bullet phrasing (achieved, delivered, led…)
Metrics — Whether your bullets contain numbers, percentages, or dollar amounts
Completeness — Whether all four sections are filled in

Aim for a score of 75 or above. 85+ is excellent.
3
Edit the Summary tab
The Summary tab contains your Professional Summary and contact details. Edit the summary to match the tone and keywords of the role. Keep it 2–3 sentences: who you are, how many years of experience, and your top 2–3 strengths. Also confirm your name, email, and phone are correct.
4
Refine your Experience bullets
Switch to the Experience tab to edit your role titles and achievement bullets. Every bullet should follow the formula:

[Action Verb] + [What you did] + [Measurable result]

Examples:
✦ Streamlined procurement process, reducing vendor onboarding time by 40%.
✦ Led cross-functional team of 8 to deliver $1.2M infrastructure upgrade on time and under budget.
5
Adjust your Skills list
On the Skills tab, add or remove skills. Each skill appears as a separate item — use the × button to remove, and + Add Skill to add. Make sure your skills mirror the language in the job description where truthful.
6
Export your résumé
Two export buttons appear below the preview:

🖨 Print / Save PDF — Opens your browser's print dialogue. Select "Save as PDF" as the destination, set paper size to A4, and save. This produces a clean, formatted résumé document.

📋 Copy Text — Copies plain text to your clipboard, useful for pasting into online application forms or Word documents.
7
Click "Find Jobs & Certs →" when ready
This button (top right of the page) moves you to the final step. You can come back to the builder at any time by pressing the Back button in the header.

Step 5 of 5
Job Search & Certifications
Search Australia's top job boards with your target role pre-filled, and explore online certifications that can boost your income potential.
1
Search the Australian job boards
Six job board cards are displayed — SEEK, Indeed Australia, LinkedIn Jobs, Jora, CareerOne, and Adzuna. Each card's Search Jobs → button opens that site with your target role and location pre-filled in a new tab. Start with SEEK, as it has the largest volume of Australian listings.
2
Review your personalised certification recommendations
Below the job boards, up to 6 certifications are shown — ranked by relevance to your target role. Each card shows:

• Income boost — estimated salary increase from holding this certification
• Duration — typical time to complete
• Cost — approximate cost in Australian dollars
• Learn More → — links directly to the official certification page
3
Read the Application Tips card
At the bottom of the page, a tips card covers key strategies for the Australian job market — including the optimal time to apply, how to follow up, how to use SEEK Salary Insights, and what to leave off an Australian résumé.
4
Use the Back button to make changes
If you want to tweak your résumé, update your target role, or re-run the Discovery Prompt, use the ← Back button in the header at any time. Your data is preserved throughout the session.
💰
Certification strategy: Prioritise certifications that are free or low-cost AND highly recognised — Google Analytics, HubSpot, AWS Cloud Practitioner, and Salesforce Trailhead are all free to complete and respected by Australian employers. Add completed certifications to your résumé's Skills or Education section immediately.

Pro Tips & FAQ
Get the Most from Elevate
Common questions and expert guidance to maximise your results.
How many keywords should I include?
Select all keywords you genuinely have experience with — typically 12–18. Don't include keywords you can't back up in an interview. Quality and honesty matter; never fabricate experience.
What ATS score should I aim for?
Aim for 75+ as a minimum. A score of 85–95 indicates excellent keyword match, strong action verb usage, measurable achievements, and a complete résumé. The score improves automatically as you add metrics and strong bullets to your experience.
Can I use Elevate for multiple job applications?
Yes — simply refresh the page to start a new session and enter the details for your next application. For best results, go through all five steps for each distinct role rather than re-using the same résumé. The biggest differentiator between candidates is customisation.
My résumé looks too long / too short — what do I do?
In Step 4, go to the Experience tab and trim bullets from older or less-relevant roles. One page is standard for under 8 years of experience. If your résumé is too short, go back to the Discovery Prompt (Step 3c) to surface more experiences, then add them to your bullets manually.
What if I don't have a job description to paste?
You can still use Elevate — just enter your target job title and industry in Step 2 and leave the description blank. Your résumé will be generated based on your profile and the template you choose, without ATS keyword optimisation. Add a JD as soon as you find one and re-run from Step 2.
Is my data saved or sent anywhere?
No. Elevate runs entirely in your browser — all data is held in memory only and is cleared when you close or refresh the page. Nothing is sent to any server. Your profile, work history, and résumé never leave your device.
How do I make the résumé look better when printed?
Use your browser's Print dialogue (Cmd + P). Set the destination to "Save as PDF", paper size to A4, margins to "Minimum" or "None", and enable "Background Graphics". This produces a clean, professional document ready for submission.
